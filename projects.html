Office Green: Increasing Brand Awareness

Objective: Use project management tools to design a structured plan to grow awareness for a plant-subscription company.

Project Overview

Office Green is a simulated project from the Google Project Management curriculum that focuses on defining a clear problem statement and turning it into a structured, trackable project. The goal is to increase brand awareness while staying within realistic time and resource constraints.

Role

As the project manager, I was responsible for clarifying goals, aligning with stakeholders, and designing a plan that could be handed off to a delivery team.

Key Deliverables

  • Project charter and high-level business case
  • Scope statement and S.M.A.R.T. goals for awareness growth
  • Stakeholder register and RACI chart
  • High-level timeline with milestones and dependencies

Methods & Tools

  • Project initiation framework from Google PM program
  • Stakeholder and communication planning
  • Risk register and basic risk-response planning
  • Gantt-style scheduling to visualize phases and milestones

What I Practiced

  • Translating vague goals into measurable outcomes
  • Balancing scope, time, and resources using the triple constraint
  • Documenting assumptions and risks up front
  • Communicating the project vision clearly to stakeholders

Next Steps

As I continue the program, I plan to expand this case study by adding mock metrics (KPIs) and additional documentation such as a sample risk log and communication plan.

Community Center Conversion

Objective: Plan the conversion of a retired middle school into a fully operational community center, including a library, garden, and support programs.

Project Overview

This conceptual project explores how to structure a community-focused initiative. The idea is to turn an unused school into a vibrant community hub that includes a public library, a community garden, flexible program spaces, and a staffing model that supports long-term operations.

Role

Acting as the project manager, I defined scope elements, success criteria, and timelines, while considering diverse stakeholders such as residents, local government, partners, and staff.

Key Deliverables

  • Draft project charter and problem statement
  • Scope definition for library, garden, and programs
  • High-level schedule (12–15 month window to full operation)
  • Outline of staffing structure and responsibilities

Methods & Tools

  • Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) for major workstreams
  • Stakeholder analysis and engagement planning
  • High-level budget and resource considerations
  • Resident survey plan for measuring community impact

Risks & Considerations

  • Funding constraints and dependency on grants or donations
  • Construction / renovation delays
  • Community alignment on priorities for programs and spaces
  • Staffing and volunteer recruitment challenges

What I Practiced

  • Thinking through both in-scope and out-of-scope items
  • Structuring work into manageable phases and milestones
  • Considering qualitative success measures (resident feedback)
  • Balancing ideal plans with realistic constraints
